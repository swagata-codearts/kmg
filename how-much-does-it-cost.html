The Vape Market

The U.S. accounts for the highest number of vapors in the market and the industry is estimated to be worth $61.4 billion by 2025. The industry has potential and if you decide you want to invest in it, proper planning would help guarantee you with definite maximum rewards. However, like any other business, it is not a straightforward feat. If you do not consider some regulations, you may regret investing in the industry.

Taxation

It is a wise decision to first check the excise taxes offered in your state for the various vaping products before you start. This is because of various states levy taxes on vaping products either as per milliliter of the liquid or a percentage of the equipment’s wholesale value.

Either way, you part with some money that affects your overall profit. Some states offer very high tax values like D.C levies 70% and Minnesota’s is 95% making them the highest across the nation.

Legal Issues

The next challenge after assessing the tax numbers is knowing every law or bylaw that governs the business. Any regulation that can limit your operation is a red flag. In 2016, the FDA classified e-cigarettes as tobacco and this extends to vaping products forcing you to adhere to any regulation for or against the equipment.

Also, various states and cities have different conditions set to acquire business permits and licenses that would actually allow you to set up a vape shop. Finally, you need to register your shop as a business entity that would guarantee protection from any lawsuit with registration costs varying in different states.

With the number of vape shops varying from state to state, California has the highest number, charging an upwards of $50 per sq. ft as a lease fee. However, some states offer a low value of even under $1 per sq. ft as a lease fee in various industrial regions. It is always advisable to prepare well and if you take a loan, make sure you have a plan to pay it back. The start-up costs may vary in different states, but you are guaranteed maximum returns should your execution be perfect. The other cost after meeting all the start-up expenses would be getting the right inventory.

The essential vape store supplies would be Vape juice, E-liquids, Replacement Coils, Hardware, and Battery chargers. Make sure you are getting quality products and not just saving on the costs.

Next, you need to determine who you are going to have as members of staff. The best people would be those with a background in retail sales and some with exceptional customer service proficiencies.

Hiring an unprofessional team may greatly affect your business and it is estimated that you may actually lose $300,000 annually if the wrong people are on your team.
